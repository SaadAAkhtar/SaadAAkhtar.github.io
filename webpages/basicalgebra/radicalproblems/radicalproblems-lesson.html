Radical Problems Section

When solving an equation involving a radical, we must first isolate the radical alone on one side. Then we raise both sides to whatever power is necessary to eliminate the radical.



When solving radical problems, you must check your solutions by plugging them back into the original equation. If they do not work, they are extraneous and must be excluded.
