Complex Fractions Section

A complex fraction is a fraction made up of two fractions. That is, there is a fraction in the numerator (top) and a fraction in the denominator (bottom).



There are two steps to handling a complex fraction. Most students remember the second, but not the first.


> Obtain a single fraction in the numerator (top) and a single fraction in the denominator (bottom).

> Invert (flip) the fraction in the denominator (bottom) and multiply it by the fraction in the numerator (top).
