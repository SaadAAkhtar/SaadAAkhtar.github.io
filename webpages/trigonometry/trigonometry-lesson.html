9.1 Trigonometric Ratios

You probably remember the mnemonic "SOH-CAH-TOA" for remembering trigonometric ratios in a right triangle. It is important to be able to set up these ratios.



9.2 Complimentary Angle Theorem

If two angles are complimentary, then the sine of the first angle and the cosine of the second angle are equal. For example:


sin(37) = cos(53)


This is easily shown in a right triangle.



If we compare sine of θ and cosine of θ − 90, we will see that they are equal.


sin(θ) = b⁄c
cos(90 - θ) = b⁄c


Warm Up Question Explanation

By the definition of cosine, cos R = RQ⁄RP and cos Z = ZY⁄ZX. Since triangle PQR is similar to triangle XYZ, with vertices P, Q, and R corresponding to vertices X, Y, and Z, respectively, the ratios RQ⁄RP and ZY⁄ZX are equal. Therefore, since cos R = RQ⁄RP = 0.263, it follows that cos Z = ZY⁄ZX = 0.263.
