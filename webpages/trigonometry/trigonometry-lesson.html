9.1 - Trigonometric Ratios

You probably remember the term "SOH-CAH-TOA" for remembering trigonometric ratios in a right triangle. It is important to be able to set up these ratios.



9.2 - Complimentary Angle Theorem

If two angles are complimentary, then the sine of the first angle and the cosine of the second angle are equal. For example:


sin(37) = cos(53)


This is easily shown in a right triangle.



If we compare sine of θ and cosine of θ − 90, we will see that they are equal.


sin(θ) = b⁄c
cos(90 - θ) = b⁄c
