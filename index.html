Saad's SAT Math Tutor Site


Introduction

This website has been created as part of a project for Georgia Institute of Technology's CS 6460: Educational Technology course. The goal of this site is to effectively teach high school students math concepts tested by the Math section of the SAT, a standardized test used by colleges and universities to judge an applicant's eligibility for admission.â€‹


Before you get started, please take a moment to read the notes below.


Regarding Lessons

There are a total of 11 math lessons denoted by black links below. However, note that "Lesson 1 - Basic Algebra" has been divided into 14 individual sections due to the size of the whole lesson. You may complete lessons in any order. However, this site will not save your progress in any regard, so be aware of where you leave off.


Regarding Warm Up Questions

These questions are easily skippable. However, it is my recommendation that you spend 5 minutes trying to solve each one even if you are unfamiliar with the math concepts present in the question. Doing so will likely help the lesson material to 'click' more quickly. Warm up questions are covered at the end of lessons and relevant sections.


Regarding Practice Questions

These questions are taken directly from official SAT tests. The formatting is largely the same as the actual questions. However, you will often see fractions represented using a '/' symbol, like so: (4 / 5). This is meant to represent a normal fraction with the '4' being the top number and the '5' being the bottom number.

You will be able to ask for hints when you get stuck. However, be aware that the hints will appear only after a certain amount of time has passed after pressing a hint button. This is to encourage you, the student, to engage with the material before turning for help. If you click the hint buttons too frequently, the amount of time until the hint appears will gradually grow. Also, only one hint countdown can be active at any given time.

You are also able to submit the practice questions whenever you please and receive immediate feedback in return. However, note that submitting too frequently will result in the submission button becoming disabled until a certain amount of time has passed. This is to, once again, encourage you, the student, to engage with the material.

Lastly, refreshing the page will result in your progress being lost. In other words, you will have to re-enter all of your answers. As such, be careful not to accidentally refresh the page.

That should be all of it. If you wish to contact me directly regarding any errors or questions, please email me at thanatosis@outlook.com. Now, please feel free to get started below!
